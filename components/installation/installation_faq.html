Garage Door Installation FAQs

Common questions homeowners ask before replacing or upgrading their garage door.

How long does it take to supply and fit a new garage door?

After your site survey and manufacturing lead time, the on‑site installation typically takes 3–5 hours. Your installer will fully test the balance, tracking and operation before sign‑off.

Will you take away and dispose of my old garage door?

Yes — dismantling, removal and environmentally responsible disposal of your old timber, steel or fibreglass door is included as part of the standard installation service.

Do new garage doors come with structural warranties?

Absolutely. Every installation includes a manufacturer’s structural warranty covering panel integrity, mechanical components (springs, cables, rollers) and, where applicable, electric motor drive systems.

Can I upgrade to an electric opener later?

Yes — most modern up‑and‑over and sectional doors are fully compatible with electric motors. If you plan to automate later, your installer can ensure the correct spring balance and hardware are fitted from day one.

Do I need planning permission for a new garage door?

In almost all cases, no. Standard garage door replacements fall under permitted development. Only structural alterations to the opening may require approval.
