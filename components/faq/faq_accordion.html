Common Garage Door Queries

Can my existing manual garage door be converted to electric?

In most cases, yes. If your current door is in good working structural condition and opens smoothly by hand, a local specialist can install a motor conversion kit to automate it. Sectional and up-and-over doors are highly compatible with automation upgrades.

How long does a typical garage door repair take?

Common repairs—such as replacing snapped cables, worn springs, or faulty remote control sensors—can usually be diagnosed and fixed within 1 to 2 hours once the technician is on-site with the correct parts.

What types of garage doors offer the best security?

Insulated roller garage doors and modern sectional doors generally provide the highest level of security. They feature interlocking designs and electronic locking mechanisms built directly into the automated motor systems, making them incredibly difficult to force open.

How much does a new garage door installation cost?

Costs vary widely depending on the style (roller, up-and-over, sectional), materials, insulation level, and whether you require automation. Local professionals offer tailored, free quotes after assessing your specific opening dimensions and requirements.

What should I do if my garage door spring or cable snaps?

If a spring or cable snaps, do not attempt to lift or operate the door. High-tension garage door mechanisms are extremely dangerous and can cause severe injury or property damage. Keep clear and request an emergency diagnostic repair visit immediately.

Are the replacement parts and mechanical repairs covered by a warranty?

Yes. All high-grade replacement components—including new high-tension springs, heavy-duty steel cables, cones, rollers, and electronic motors—fitted by vetted local experts come with explicit parts and labor warranties for structural peace of mind.

How often should a garage door be inspected or serviced?

To maximize lifespan, tracking balance, and safety, a residential garage door should ideally undergo a basic structural check and lubrication service every 12 to 18 months. Regular maintenance prevents unexpected spring and cable failures.

How does an electric automated garage door operate during a power cut?

All modern automated electric motor tracks feature an internal manual release mechanism, typically operated by a pull-cord. Pulling this handle safely disconnects the motor car from the door, allowing you to lift it open easily by hand until power returns.
