Garage Door Automation FAQs

Can any garage door be converted to electric?

Most standard up-and-over, sectional, and side-hinged garage doors can be successfully retrofitted with an electric motor system, provided the existing structural frame and tracking system are stable and in good working alignment.

What happens to the electric door during a power cut?

Every automated motor setup comes equipped with a manual internal release cord. Pulling this cord detaches the door from the carriage track, allowing you to lift it smoothly by hand until power is fully restored. External release locks can also be fitted if your garage has no other entry door.

How secure are automated garage door motors?

Automated conversions significantly increase security. Modern electric openers act as an automatic positive lock, holding the door firmly shut down against the floor. They also utilise rolling-code wireless technology, preventing thieves from copying or cloning your remote controller signal.
